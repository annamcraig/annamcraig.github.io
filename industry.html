Industry Projects

Curi Bio

Fall 2020 - Spring 2021

This past year I was lucky enough to work as a software engineer at CuriBio, a biotechnology company creating solutions for improving therapeutic outcomes for muscular diseases. At CuriBio, I developed methods for analysis, storage and visualization for biological data. Additionally, I optimized the storage of files within each repository by developing a file trimming method, to keep only pertainit sections of a given file. As issues with current customers arose, I communicated directly with customers to diagnose the problem and solive it efficiently. Throughout my time at Curi, I became confident at presenting my contributions at weekly sprint meetings and engaged in paired programming to write high quality code.

PruneTrain

Winter 2021 - Spring 2021

As apart of the iSchool at the University of Washington, I participated in an industry project with Microsoft, as the team lead. The goal of the project was to decrease carbon emissions by reducing training time for models through extending PruneTrain work. In doing so I facilitated weekly meetings to present the groups progress to our Microsoft mentors, acted as a liaison between mentors and the group, delegated tasks and maintained our designated timeline. Additionally, I implemented an API for the PruneTrain algorithm to create a public facing sample and ran experiments on Azure while implementing the algorithm on various datasets such as Cifar and ImageNet.

Intellectual Ventures

Summer 2019

I served as a biotechnology associate intern within the E. coli diagnostics development team for the Global Good Fund at Intellectual Ventures. To increase the long-term stability of bacteriophages for device field testing and use, I developed a study that evaluated the impact of the addition of additives to the stability of bacteriophage in liquid over eight weeks. Throughout the project, I designed a liquid assay assessment to measure bacteriophage reporter ouput, to be analyzed via excel, and conducted titer experiments as a secondary assessment. In addition, I presented research results to senior scientists, managers and directors to support product development in the company.
